People / Partner

Joel Ezekiel Nwushie, Esq.

Runs the chambers’ disputes work on the view that the first duty of a litigator is arithmetic: what the claim is worth, what pursuing it costs, and whether the fight serves the client at all.

About

Joel appears in commercial and property litigation before Nigerian courts and in arbitration under the Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023. His cases are built on documents — the evidentiary spine assembled before the first process is filed — and his advice includes the sentence most litigators avoid: “this one is not worth it.”

He works closely with the commercial practice on dispute-resolution clauses, on the theory that the cheapest arbitration is the one your contract made unnecessary.
